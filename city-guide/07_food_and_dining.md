Food & Dining

Driebergen-Rijsenburg is a village, not a city, but the food scene is better than its size suggests. The proximity to Utrecht and the conference trade bring culinary ambition to an otherwise quiet place.

## Restaurants

**Restaurant Kontrast** at Landgoed de Horst serves seasonal dishes built around regional ingredients. The philosophy is "echt eten" — real food that connects to the landscape. The setting looks out over the estate grounds, and the menu changes with what is available locally. Good for a composed dinner after a day of workshops.

**Brasserie de Wissel** on the Hoofdstraat is a village brasserie with a straightforward Dutch-French menu. Steak, fish, seasonal vegetables, and a solid wine list. Unpretentious and reliable.

**Restaurant het Kampje** on the Traaij is a small neighborhood restaurant with an emphasis on fresh, locally sourced ingredients and a regularly changing menu. Intimate setting with a small terrace.

**De Bistronoom** in nearby Zeist is worth the short cycle ride for more ambitious cooking — creative small plates, natural wines, and a relaxed atmosphere in a former bank building.

## Dutch Food Traditions

Traditional Dutch food you may encounter:
- **Stamppot**: mashed potatoes mixed with vegetables (boerenkool, hutspot, zuurkool), served with rookworst (smoked sausage)
- **Bitterballen**: deep-fried beef ragout balls, standard bar snack with mustard
- **Pannenkoeken**: Dutch pancakes, thinner than American but thicker than French crêpes
- **Haring**: raw herring with onion and pickle, eaten by holding the fish by the tail (seasonal, from June)
- **Appeltaart**: Dutch apple cake, denser and spicier than American apple pie, often with whipped cream (slagroom)

## Farm Shops and Local Products

The Heuvelrug has several farm shops (boerderijwinkels) selling local produce: eggs, honey, fruit from orchards along the Langbroekerwetering, and seasonal vegetables. **Landwinkel de Woerdt** near Doorn and several smaller operations along the Langbroekerwetering road are worth seeking out.

## Supermarkets

For self-catering, Driebergen has a **Jumbo** and an **Albert Heijn** within walking distance of the center. Both are open daily.
