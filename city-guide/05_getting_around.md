Getting Around

Driebergen-Rijsenburg is small enough to cover on foot or by bike, and the surrounding Heuvelrug is best explored on two wheels.

## Cycling

This is the Netherlands, and the bicycle is king. Driebergen has dedicated cycle paths (fietspaden) connecting all parts of the village to the station, shops, and surrounding forests. The region is part of the national cycling junction network (fietsknooppunten), which lets you plan routes by following numbered signs at intersections.

Popular cycling routes from Driebergen:
- **Driebergen → Austerlitz → Pyramide**: 8 km through deep forest, mostly unpaved but smooth sandy paths
- **Driebergen → Doorn → Amerongen**: 15 km along the ridge with views over the Rhine valley
- **Driebergen → Zeist → Slot Zeist**: 6 km on paved cycle paths through residential areas and parkland

OV-fiets (public transport bikes) are available at Driebergen-Zeist station for €4.55 per day with a valid OV-chipkaart and personal subscription.

## Walking

The forests surrounding Driebergen have an extensive network of marked walking trails maintained by Staatsbosbeheer (national forestry) and Utrechts Landschap (provincial trust). Most trails are well-signed with colored posts or mushroom-shaped route markers.

Key walking areas accessible from the village center:
- **Heidestein-Bornia**: directly north, 5 minutes from the Hoofdstraat
- **Sparrendaal**: 10-minute walk east along the Arnhemse Bovenweg
- **Ridderoordse Bos**: south of Rijsenburg, connecting to the Langbroekerwetering

## By Car

For distances beyond cycling range — day trips to Utrecht city center, Amersfoort, or the Veluwe — a car is convenient. Parking in the village is free and generally easy. The A12 motorway is three minutes from the village edge.

## Public Transport

Bus line 50 connects Driebergen to Zeist and Utrecht Science Park. Frequency is every 15–30 minutes on weekdays, less on weekends. For most visitors, the train is more useful than the bus.

## Taxi and Ride Services

Local taxi services operate from the station. Uber is available but response times are longer than in Utrecht or Amsterdam. For short trips within the village, cycling is almost always faster.
