Museums & Art

Driebergen itself has no major museum, but the Utrechtse Heuvelrug and surrounding area offer several worthwhile cultural destinations within easy cycling or driving distance.

## Huis Doorn (Museum Kaiser Wilhelm)

In neighboring Doorn, Huis Doorn is the manor where Kaiser Wilhelm II of Germany lived in exile from 1920 until his death in 1941. The house is preserved largely as he left it, with original furnishings, personal objects, and the garden he tended obsessively. The museum provides an unusual window into the end of European monarchy and the interwar period. Open Wednesday through Sunday.

## Kasteel Amerongen

At the southeastern end of the municipality, Kasteel Amerongen is one of the few fully furnished castle museums in the Netherlands. Destroyed by fire in 2007 and meticulously restored, it displays four centuries of aristocratic life on the Heuvelrug. The surrounding park and orangery are open for walking.

## Slot Zeist

In neighboring Zeist, the Slot (palace) was built in the seventeenth century and later became the center of a Moravian Brotherhood community. The building and its formal gardens are architecturally significant. The surrounding Broedergemeente neighborhood, with its symmetrical layout, is unique in the Netherlands.

## Museum Oud Amelisweerd

Between Bunnik and Utrecht, this small museum in the Amelisweerd estate shows exhibitions of contemporary art in a historical country house setting. The estate grounds along the Kromme Rijn river are beautiful for walking.

## Art in the Landscape

The Heuvelrug has a tradition of art in nature. Sculptures appear along forest paths, and several estates host temporary exhibitions in their gardens during summer months. The annual Beelden in de Tuin (Sculptures in the Garden) events rotate between venues.

## Utrecht Museums

For a full museum day, Utrecht city center (10 minutes by train) offers:
- **Centraal Museum**: Dutch art from medieval to modern, including the largest Rietveld collection
- **Museum Speelklok**: mechanical musical instruments in a medieval church
- **DOMunder**: archaeological experience beneath the Dom square
- **Miffy Museum (nijntje museum)**: for families, dedicated to Dick Bruna's iconic creation
