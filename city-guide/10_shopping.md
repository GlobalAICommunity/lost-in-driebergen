Shopping

Driebergen-Rijsenburg has a compact village center with independent shops, a weekly market, and enough for daily needs. For serious retail, Utrecht is ten minutes away by train.

## The Hoofdstraat

The Hoofdstraat is the main shopping street, running through the village center. It has a mix of independent shops, a few chain stores, and service businesses. Notable stops:

- **Bakkerij Nap**: the village bakery, good for bread, pastries, and vlaai
- **Boekhandel Veenendaal**: independent bookshop with a curated Dutch and English selection
- **Drogisterij de Heuvelrug**: traditional Dutch drugstore with health products, cosmetics, and household items
- **Several fashion boutiques**: small independent clothing shops with curated selections

## The Saturday Market

Every Saturday morning, a market sets up on the Traaij with stalls selling:
- Fresh vegetables and fruit (often from the Betuwe region)
- Cheese (Gouda, aged varieties, goat cheese)
- Fish (herring, kibbeling, smoked eel)
- Flowers and plants
- Bread and stroopwafels

The market is small but genuinely local. Arrive before 11:00 for the best selection.

## Farm Shops

The agricultural land south of the ridge, along the Langbroekerwetering, supports several farm shops:
- Seasonal fruit (cherries, apples, pears from local orchards)
- Fresh eggs, honey, and preserves
- Asparagus in spring (a Dutch obsession from late April through June)

## Zeist and Utrecht

For more shopping options, Zeist has a larger pedestrian shopping center (Slotlaan and surroundings) with more variety. Utrecht's city center — especially the Vredenburg area, Twijnstraat, and Oudegracht — has everything from international chains to independent design shops, all ten minutes away by train.
