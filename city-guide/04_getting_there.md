Getting There

Driebergen-Rijsenburg is well connected by rail and road, sitting at the intersection of major transport corridors in the center of the Netherlands.

## By Train

**Driebergen-Zeist station** is on the Utrecht–Arnhem intercity line. Trains run every 15 minutes in both directions during the day:

- **Utrecht Centraal**: 10 minutes by intercity, 14 minutes by sprinter
- **Arnhem Centraal**: 35 minutes by intercity
- **Amsterdam Centraal**: 40 minutes (change at Utrecht Centraal)
- **Schiphol Airport**: 50 minutes (change at Utrecht Centraal)

The station is a five-minute cycle or fifteen-minute walk from the village center. It has covered bike parking (stalling) with space for over a thousand bicycles.

## By Car

Driebergen sits between two major motorways:

- **A12** (Utrecht–Arnhem): Exit 20 (Driebergen) puts you on the Arnhemse Bovenweg, which leads directly into the village. Travel time from Utrecht: approximately 15 minutes.
- **A28** (Utrecht–Amersfoort): Exit Maarn/Doorn is a ten-minute drive from Driebergen through the forest.

From Schiphol Airport, the drive is approximately 50 minutes via the A2 and A12.

## By Bus

Regional bus services connect Driebergen to surrounding towns:

- **Line 50** (U-OV): Driebergen – Zeist – Bilthoven – Utrecht Science Park
- **Line 56** (U-OV): Driebergen – Doorn – Amerongen – Rhenen

Buses stop at Driebergen-Zeist station and at several points along the Hoofdstraat.

## To Landgoed de Horst

From Driebergen-Zeist station, Landgoed de Horst is a 10-minute cycle ride or 25-minute walk through residential streets and forest paths. By car from the A12, follow exit 20 toward Driebergen and continue to De Horst 1. The estate has its own parking area.
