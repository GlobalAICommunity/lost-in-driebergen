Knowledge & Innovation

The Heuvelrug area has a quiet but notable connection to knowledge work, education, and technology — largely through its proximity to Utrecht and the tradition of using estates as conference and learning venues.

## Conference and Retreat Tradition

Driebergen's estates have a long history as venues for thinking, meeting, and training. Landgoed de Horst, the Volkshogeschool (folk high school) tradition, and various retreat centers have hosted everything from corporate strategy sessions to academic workshops for decades. This tradition of "going to the woods to think" predates the modern tech industry but fits perfectly with it.

## Utrecht Science Park (De Uithof)

Fifteen minutes by bus (line 50) or twenty minutes by bike, Utrecht Science Park is one of the largest concentrations of research and education in the Netherlands. Utrecht University, UMC Utrecht (university hospital), and numerous research institutes are located here. The campus also hosts tech companies and startups in the Utrecht Inc. incubator.

## Utrecht as Tech Hub

Utrecht has grown into a significant Dutch tech hub, smaller than Amsterdam but with strong clusters in gaming, health tech, and AI. The city hosts various tech meetups, and companies like Booking.com (offices), Mendix (low-code platform, founded in Rotterdam but with Utrecht connections), and numerous startups operate in the region.

## Digital Infrastructure

The Netherlands has among the best digital infrastructure in Europe. The Amsterdam Internet Exchange (AMS-IX) and multiple data centers ensure low latency. For anyone working remotely or running workshops from Driebergen, connectivity is excellent — fiber internet is standard even in this village setting.

## Conference Venues

Beyond Landgoed de Horst, the area offers several meeting and workshop venues:
- **International Theatre Amsterdam** retreat space (Zeist area)
- **Antropia** in Driebergen: a venue for courses and cultural events
- **Slot Zeist**: event space in the historical palace

The combination of forest quiet, good transport connections, and professional venues makes the Heuvelrug a natural choice for off-site meetings and workshops.
