Day Trips

Driebergen's central location in the Netherlands makes it an excellent base for day trips. The train network connects you to several cities within 30–60 minutes.

## Utrecht (10 minutes by train)

The fourth-largest city in the Netherlands deserves a full day. Highlights:
- **Dom Tower**: the tallest church tower in the Netherlands (112m), climb it for panoramic views
- **Oudegracht**: the unique two-level canal with shops and restaurants in the former wharves below street level
- **Centraal Museum**: Dutch art, Rietveld furniture, and local history
- **Museum Speelklok**: mechanical musical instruments in a medieval church
- **Trajectum Lumen**: evening light art walk along the canals
- **Shopping**: Twijnstraat for independent shops, Vredenburg for larger stores

## Amersfoort (20 minutes by train)

A beautifully preserved medieval city with:
- **Koppelpoort**: a medieval water gate spanning the river, one of the best-preserved in the Netherlands
- **Onze Lieve Vrouwetoren**: a church tower in the old city center
- **Mondriaan's birthplace**: the painter Piet Mondrian was born here; a small museum marks the spot
- **Compact old city center**: walkable, attractive, with good cafés and restaurants

## Kasteel de Haar (30 minutes by car)

Near Haarzuilens, west of Utrecht, Kasteel de Haar is the largest castle in the Netherlands. Built in neo-Gothic style in the 1890s by architect Pierre Cuypers (who also designed Amsterdam Centraal station), it looks like a fairy tale and the grounds are extensive. Open daily.

## Rhenen and Ouwehands Dierenpark (25 minutes by car)

Ouwehands Zoo in Rhenen is best known for its giant panda exhibit (the only one in the Netherlands) and its bear sanctuary. Rhenen itself sits on the edge of the Heuvelrug where it meets the Rhine, with views from the Cunera church tower over the river valley.

## The Veluwe (45 minutes by car)

The largest natural area in the Netherlands: vast heathland, forests, and sand drifts north of Arnhem. Key destinations:
- **Nationaal Park De Hoge Veluwe**: cycling through the park on free white bikes, the Kröller-Müller Museum (major Van Gogh collection), and dramatic landscapes
- **Paleis Het Loo** (Apeldoorn): the former royal palace with formal gardens

## Gouda (45 minutes by train)

Famous for cheese but worth visiting for the medieval town hall, Sint-Janskerk (longest church in the Netherlands with 72 stained glass windows), and the cheese market on Thursday mornings in summer.
