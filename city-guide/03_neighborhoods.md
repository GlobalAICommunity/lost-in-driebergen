Neighborhoods & Surrounding Villages

The municipality of Utrechtse Heuvelrug stretches across the glacial ridge from northwest to southeast. Each village has its own character, but they share the same landscape of forests, heather, and sandy paths.

## Driebergen Centrum

The heart of Driebergen is compact and walkable. The Hoofdstraat and Traaij form the main axes, lined with shops, cafés, and the occasional estate gate peeking between buildings. The Reformed church tower marks the center. Most daily needs are met within a few hundred meters.

## Rijsenburg

South of the railway line, Rijsenburg is quieter and more residential. It has its own church (Catholic, reflecting its historical identity), a small cluster of shops along the Arnhemse Bovenweg, and a gentle transition into the open polderland of the Langbroekerwetering.

## Austerlitz

East of Driebergen, Austerlitz sits in deep forest. Named after Napoleon's 1805 victory — French troops were garrisoned here and built a pyramid that still stands — the village has an isolated, almost frontier feel. The Pyramide van Austerlitz is a popular walking destination and regional landmark.

## Doorn

South along the ridge, Doorn is known for Huis Doorn, the manor where Kaiser Wilhelm II of Germany lived in exile from 1920 until his death in 1941. The house is now a museum. Doorn has a pleasant village center with its own shops and restaurants, and direct forest connections to Driebergen.

## Leersum

Further southeast, Leersum offers dramatic views from the Darthuizerpoort and the Kaapse Bossen. The landscape here is hillier and more open, with heathland and scattered Scots pine. Good starting point for longer hikes and mountain bike routes.

## Maarn

Northwest of Driebergen, Maarn is a small residential village on the railway line between Driebergen-Zeist and Amersfoort. It borders the Maarnse Berg nature reserve and has a quiet, leafy character similar to Driebergen but on a smaller scale.

## Amerongen

At the southeastern end of the municipality, Amerongen sits where the ridge meets the Rhine floodplain. Kasteel Amerongen, restored after a 2007 fire, is one of the finest castle museums in the Netherlands. The Amerongse Berg offers one of the highest points on the Heuvelrug.

## Zeist

Technically a separate municipality to the northwest, Zeist is Driebergen's closest urban neighbor. It has a larger shopping center, the Slot Zeist (a Moravian-built palace), and a well-connected bus network. The border between Driebergen and Zeist is invisible in the forest.
