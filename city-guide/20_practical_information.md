Practical Information

Essential practical details for visitors to Driebergen-Rijsenburg and the Utrechtse Heuvelrug.

## Language

Dutch is the official language. English is spoken fluently by the vast majority of the population. German is widely understood, especially in this eastern part of Utrecht province. French is less common outside tourism professionals.

## Currency

The Euro (€). Card payment is universal — most shops, restaurants, and even market stalls accept debit cards (PIN) and contactless payments. Some small businesses do not accept credit cards (Visa/Mastercard); look for signs. Cash is accepted everywhere but increasingly unnecessary.

## Tipping

Tipping is not obligatory in the Netherlands. Service charge is included in listed prices. Rounding up or leaving 5–10% for good restaurant service is appreciated but not expected. At bars: rounding up to the nearest euro is common.

## Weather

The Netherlands has a maritime climate: mild, wet, and changeable.
- **Spring** (March–May): 8–18°C, variable, April showers are real
- **Summer** (June–August): 18–25°C typical, occasional heat waves above 30°C
- **Autumn** (September–November): 8–16°C, beautiful forest colors, fog mornings
- **Winter** (December–February): 0–7°C, grey and damp, rare snow

Always carry a rain jacket or small umbrella. The weather can change within an hour. The forest provides shelter from both rain and wind.

## Public Transport (OV-chipkaart)

The OV-chipkaart is the national public transport smartcard. You can:
- Buy a disposable card at the station for single journeys
- Use a personal OV-chipkaart (requires Dutch bank account) for all public transport
- Use contactless debit/credit card directly on train gates and bus readers (OVpay)

Check-in and check-out at the gates or card readers on every journey. Failure to check out results in a maximum fare charge.

## Health and Emergencies

- **Emergency number**: 112 (police, fire, ambulance)
- **Non-emergency police**: 0900-8844
- **GP (huisarts)**: For non-emergency medical issues. Walk-in clinics are not common; most GPs work by appointment. Hotels can advise.
- **Pharmacy (apotheek)**: There is a pharmacy in Driebergen centrum on the Hoofdstraat.
- **Hospital**: nearest is St. Antonius Ziekenhuis in Utrecht or Meander Medisch Centrum in Amersfoort.

## Water

Tap water in the Netherlands is excellent quality and perfectly safe to drink. No need for bottled water.

## Electricity

Standard European plugs (Type C/F), 230V/50Hz. UK and US devices need an adapter.

## Cycling Safety

- Always use bike lights after dark (front white, rear red) — police fine for this
- Lock your bike with the built-in ring lock AND a chain lock to a fixed object
- Yield to traffic from the right at unmarked intersections
- Stay in dedicated bike lanes (fietspad) where they exist
- Hand signals for turning are expected

## Shops and Opening Hours

- Supermarkets: typically 8:00–21:00 or 22:00
- Village shops: typically 9:00/10:00–17:30, closed Sunday or limited Sunday hours
- Saturday market: approximately 8:00–13:00

## Quiet Hours

The Netherlands takes quiet hours seriously in residential areas. Keep noise down after 22:00, especially on weeknights. The village atmosphere is genuinely quiet — respect it.
