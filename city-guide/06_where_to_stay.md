Where to Stay

Driebergen-Rijsenburg and the surrounding Heuvelrug offer accommodation ranging from estate hotels to forest campsites.

## Estate Hotels

**Landgoed de Horst** on De Horst 1 is a conference and hotel estate set in mature forest. It has 113 hotel rooms, restaurant Kontrast, and conference facilities including the Maitland congress hall. The setting is secluded — surrounded by trees on all sides — yet only ten minutes by bike from the station.

**Hotel Lagerweij** in Austerlitz is a small boutique hotel in a renovated farmhouse, deep in the forest near the Pyramide. Good for those who want total quiet.

## Zeist Options

Nearby Zeist has additional hotels including **Hotel Figi**, a larger conference hotel with spa facilities on the Het Rond square, and **Buitenplaats Sparrendaal**, a smaller option in a historical estate.

## Bed & Breakfast

Several B&Bs operate in the village and surrounding area. These tend to be in renovated estate outbuildings or private homes with forest views. Check local listings as availability changes seasonally.

## Camping and Nature Stays

**Camping Bos Park Bilthoven** is northwest of Driebergen in the same forest belt. For a more rustic option, Staatsbosbeheer operates primitive campsites (paalkampeerplaatsen) in the forest where you can pitch a tent at a numbered post with minimal facilities.

## Practical Tips

- Book estate hotels well in advance during conference season (spring and autumn)
- The area is quiet on weekends; restaurants may have limited hours
- Driebergen-Zeist station is the most useful landmark for navigation — most accommodations reference their distance from it
