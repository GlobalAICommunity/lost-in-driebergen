Introduction

Driebergen-Rijsenburg sits on the wooded spine of the Utrechtse Heuvelrug, a glacial ridge that runs diagonally across the province of Utrecht. It is a village of estates hidden behind beech lanes, morning cyclists disappearing into forest paths, and a quietness that feels earned rather than empty. The trees outnumber the residents by an absurd margin, and every season reshapes the landscape completely: white frost on heather in January, bluebells carpeting the forest floor in May, copper canopies arching over bike paths in October.

The village has always attracted people who wanted to be close to the country without being far from the city. In the nineteenth century, wealthy merchants built their country houses here along the sandy ridge. In the twentieth century, conference centers and retreat venues discovered the same appeal. Today, commuters live ten minutes by train from Utrecht Centraal yet step off the platform into a place where the loudest sound is often a woodpecker.

This guide covers Driebergen-Rijsenburg and the surrounding Utrechtse Heuvelrug municipality: its forests and estates, its food and village life, its connections to Utrecht and Amersfoort, and the hidden corners that reward those who leave the main roads. Whether you are here for a workshop, a weekend, or just passing through, the Heuvelrug will give you more than you expect.
