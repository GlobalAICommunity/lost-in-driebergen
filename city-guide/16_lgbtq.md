Inclusivity & Community

The Netherlands has a strong tradition of tolerance and progressive social policies. While village life on the Heuvelrug is quieter than Amsterdam or Utrecht, the values of inclusivity and openness are broadly shared.

## LGBTQ+ in the Netherlands

The Netherlands was the first country in the world to legalize same-sex marriage (2001). Anti-discrimination protections are comprehensive and widely enforced. Same-sex couples are visible and accepted in daily life throughout the country, including in smaller communities.

## Village Character

Driebergen-Rijsenburg is a relatively affluent, well-educated community. The social atmosphere is tolerant but reserved — characteristic of Dutch village life outside the Randstad. People tend to be polite and private rather than demonstratively welcoming, but genuine friendliness surfaces quickly in shops, cafés, and on forest paths.

## Utrecht LGBTQ+ Scene

For an active LGBTQ+ social scene, Utrecht is ten minutes away by train. The city has dedicated bars, cultural events, and an annual Canal Pride (Utrecht Pride) that runs through the historic canals. The atmosphere is welcoming and less tourism-driven than Amsterdam's scene.

## Accessibility

Most public buildings, restaurants, and hotels in Driebergen are accessible by Dutch building standards. Forest paths vary — the main routes are generally suitable for wheelchairs and mobility aids, but unpaved sandy tracks can be challenging. Driebergen-Zeist station has step-free access to all platforms.

## Language

English is widely spoken in the Netherlands and certainly in the Heuvelrug area. Most residents under 60 speak English comfortably. Shop staff, restaurant servers, and hotel personnel will switch to English without hesitation. Learning a few Dutch words (dank je wel, alstublieft, goedemorgen) is appreciated but never required.
