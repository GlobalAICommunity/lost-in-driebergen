Hidden Gems

The Heuvelrug rewards those who leave the obvious paths. These are places and experiences that rarely appear in tourist listings but define what makes this area special.

## The Tunnel of Trees on Overbergweg

Between Driebergen and Leersum, the Overbergweg is a narrow lane completely enclosed by overhanging beech trees. In summer it forms a green tunnel; in autumn, a golden cathedral. Best experienced by bike early in the morning when the light filters through.

## Theehuis Heidestein

A traditional forest tearoom at the crossroads in the Heidestein nature reserve, north of Driebergen. Pancakes, coffee, and apple cake served on a terrace surrounded by trees. No pretension, no Wi-Fi signal to speak of. Cash preferred. The kind of place you stumble upon and remember years later.

## The Ice Age Boulders (Zwerfstenen)

Scattered through the forests are glacial erratics — boulders carried from Scandinavia by the same ice sheets that created the Heuvelrug. They sit incongruously among beech and birch trees, smooth and foreign. A few are marked with information signs; others you simply find.

## Langbroekerwetering Cycling Route

The narrow road along the medieval Langbroekerwetering canal, south of the ridge, passes through a landscape of orchards, small farms, and estate grounds that feels unchanged from a previous century. Cherry blossoms in April, fruit stands in summer, and barely any traffic at any time.

## The Sunken Lanes (Holle Wegen)

Water erosion over centuries has carved sunken lanes into the sandy ridge — paths that run below the surrounding land surface between high banks covered in moss and tree roots. Several exist in the forest between Driebergen and Austerlitz. Walk through one at dusk for an atmospheric experience.

## Kasteel Drakestein Exterior

Between Lage Vuursche and Baarn, slightly north of the Heuvelrug, Kasteel Drakestein was the residence of Princess Beatrix before she became Queen. The castle itself is private and not open to visitors, but the surrounding forest and the approach lane offer a glimpse of this hidden royal estate.

## The Dawn Chorus (May)

Set an alarm for 4:30am in early May and walk into the Heidestein forest. The dawn chorus — dozens of bird species singing simultaneously as the light returns — is one of the most powerful natural experiences available within walking distance of the village. Bring coffee.

## Old Railway Path to Austerlitz

A former narrow-gauge railway line between Driebergen and Austerlitz has been converted into a walking and cycling path. The route runs dead straight through the forest, and the gentle engineering curves and consistent width reveal its industrial heritage to anyone paying attention.
