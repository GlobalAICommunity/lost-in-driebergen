Festivals & Events

Village life on the Heuvelrug follows a seasonal rhythm of markets, celebrations, and community events. Driebergen is not a festival city, but what it does, it does with genuine local participation.

## Koningsdag (King's Day) — April 27

The national holiday. In Driebergen, the celebration centers on the Traaij and Hoofdstraat with a vrijmarkt (free market) where anyone can sell anything from a blanket on the ground, live music, children's games, and orange decorations everywhere. The atmosphere is relaxed and communal. Arrive early for the best finds at the market.

## Kermis (Village Fair)

The annual kermis comes to Driebergen in summer with a small fairground on the Traaij: spinning rides, game stalls, oliebollen (even out of season), and the sweet smell of sugar spinning. It runs for a few days and draws the whole village.

## Saturday Market

Every Saturday morning, the weekly market on the Traaij brings fresh produce, cheese, fish, flowers, and stroopwafels to the village center. It is as much a social event as a shopping trip — neighbors meet, news travels, and the coffee terraces fill up.

## Sinterklaas Arrival — mid-November

The arrival of Sinterklaas by steamboat (or sometimes horse and carriage in inland villages) marks the start of the December gift-giving season. Children line the streets, there are pepernoten scattered everywhere, and the village center takes on a festive atmosphere distinct from the Christmas decorations that follow.

## Open Monumentendag — Second Weekend of September

National heritage weekend when historical buildings, estates, and private properties open their doors to the public for free. In Driebergen, this is an excellent opportunity to see the interiors of estates and villas normally closed to visitors.

## Musical and Cultural Events

**Antropia** in Driebergen hosts concerts, lectures, and cultural evenings throughout the year. The programming tends toward classical music, jazz, and spoken word. Check their calendar for what is on during your visit.

## Forest Events

Various organized walks, runs, and cycling events use the Heuvelrug forests as their course. The **Heuvelrug Trail** and **Heuvelrug Marathon** draw trail runners from across the country. Check seasonal listings for dates.
