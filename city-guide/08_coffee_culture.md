Coffee Culture

The Dutch run on coffee. The Netherlands has one of the highest per-capita coffee consumption rates in the world, and even a small village like Driebergen reflects this. Expect good coffee in unexpected places.

## Village Coffee Spots

**Bakkerij Nap** on the Hoofdstraat is the village institution — a bakery that has been operating for generations. The coffee is solid, the vlaai (fruit tart) is excellent, and the terras on the street fills up on market mornings. Go early on Saturday for the best pastries.

**Lunchroom het Zusje** on the Traaij is a cozy lunch café with proper espresso, fresh sandwiches, and homemade cakes. Popular with locals for a mid-morning stop. Small terrace with a view of the village street.

**Grand Café de Tram** near the station is a good option if you arrive by train and want a quick coffee before heading into the village. Named after the historical tram line that once connected Driebergen to Zeist.

## Specialty Coffee

The best specialty coffee near Driebergen is in Zeist and Utrecht. **Man Met Bril Koffie** in Utrecht (two locations) is one of the best roasters in the province. **Koffie & Ik** in Zeist on Slotlaan serves carefully prepared filter and espresso from rotating guest roasters.

## The Terras Culture

Dutch terras (terrace) culture is central to village life. When the sun comes out — even briefly — tables appear on sidewalks and in courtyards, and people sit with coffee, cake, and conversation. In Driebergen, the Hoofdstraat terraces catch the afternoon sun. The unwritten rule: linger as long as you like, but order something every hour or so.

## Tea and Alternatives

**Theehuis Heidestein** in the forest north of Driebergen is a traditional theehuis (tearoom) at a forest crossroads. It serves tea, coffee, pancakes, and simple lunches to walkers and cyclists. The building is rustic, the setting is pure forest, and it feels like stepping into a different decade. Cash preferred.
